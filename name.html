How did it get it's name?

During the Renaissance, megalaodon teeth were believed to be petrified tongues (glossopetrae) of dragons and snakes. However, in 1667 naturalist Nicolas Steno correctly identified them as shark teeth. His sketches (from his book The Head of a Shark Dissected) of the assumed shark head and teeth are seen to the right. Swiss naturalist Louis Agassiz (pictured below) gave it its initial scientific name, Carcharodon megalodon in 1843. Over time, it's name was updated on several occasions. While it's full scientifc name is still up for debate, it's Ancient Greek translation is unanmiously agreed upon. The name megalodon is Ancient Greek for "big tooth". Many nicknames were given to the creature including: "giant tooth shark", "megatooth shark", "big tooth shark", or "Meg" (for short).
